Macroscopic fluid motion is due to body forces, typically gravity, and
is affected primarily through density changes. These changes may be
influenced by differing species concentrations or temperature.

\section{Flat Plate Boundary Layers in Natural
Convection}\label{flat-plate-boundary-layers-in-natural-convection}

Introduce the isothermal compressibility coefficient and coefficient of
thermal expansion as

\begin{equation}\protect\phantomsection\label{eq-coefficient-definitions}{
\begin{align*}
K & =\frac 1{\rho}\left(\frac {\partial\rho}{\partial P}\right)_T \\
\beta & =-\frac 1{\beta}\left(\frac {\partial\rho}{\partial T}\right)_P
\end{align*}
}\end{equation}

The isothermal compressibility \(K\) term is neglected when deriving
these equations, but is important enough to warrant mentioning it
nevertheless. The conservation equations governing free convection is

\begin{equation}\protect\phantomsection\label{eq-conservation-equations}{
\begin{gather*}
\frac {\partial u}{\partial x}+\frac {\partial v}{\partial y}=0 \\
u\frac {\partial u}{\partial x}+v\frac {\partial u}{\partial y}=-g_x\beta(T-T_{\infty})-\frac 1{\rho}\left(\frac {\mathrm dP}{\mathrm dx}-\frac {\mathrm dP_{\infty}}{\mathrm dx}\right)+\nu\frac {\partial^2u}{\partial y^2} \\
\rho C_p\left(u\frac {\partial T}{\partial x}+v\frac {\partial T}{\partial y}\right)=k\frac {\partial^2T}{\partial y^2}
\end{gather*}
}\end{equation}
